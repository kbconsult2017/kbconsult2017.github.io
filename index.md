# About Me

Hello! I am a Ph.D. candidate in Biostatistics at the [University of Nebraska Medical Center](https://www.unmc.edu/). I expect to defend in 2026 and am on the academic and research-institution job market.

My research develops scalable Bayesian methods for spatio-temporal data, motivated by problems in environmental health. My dissertation introduces the *SVD-PICAR* framework — a family of compressed basis-function methods that make hierarchical Bayesian spatio-temporal models computationally practical at large scale. I extend the framework to Gaussian, multinomial, and count outcomes, and to Bayesian Kernel Machine Regression for estimating the health effects of complex environmental mixtures.

As a Graduate Research Assistant in the [UNMC Water, Climate and Health Program](https://www.unmc.edu/publichealth/research/multidisciplinary-programs/wch/index.html), I work at the intersection of statistics, climate, and public health, building models that connect environmental exposures to health outcomes. Before my doctoral studies I earned an M.Sc. in Mathematics from the [University of Cincinnati](https://www.uc.edu/), an M.Sc. in Statistics and Mathematics from [East Tennessee State University](https://www.etsu.edu/ehome/), and a B.Sc. in Mathematical Science (Statistics and Computer Science) from the [University of Ghana](https://www.ug.edu.gh/).

### Research Interests

- Bayesian statistics; hierarchical Bayesian modeling
- Spatial and spatio-temporal statistics
- Bayesian Kernel Machine Regression
- Dimension reduction for large spatio-temporal models
- Causal inference
- Environmental health statistics
- Missing data methods
